Pookie Tools — Free Online Delimiter Tool & Duplicate Finder

Pookie Tools is a free browser-based utility for developers, data analysts, and writers who need to quickly split or join text with a delimiter, or find and remove duplicate entries from any list.

No account required. No data is uploaded. Everything runs instantly in your browser.

Key Features

  • Delimiter Converter — Split delimited text into lines, or join lines with comma, semicolon, pipe, slash, equals, or any custom character.
  • Bidirectional — Convert forward or reverse with one click.
  • Duplicate Finder — Paste any list and instantly see duplicates, unique values, or a fully deduplicated list.
  • Smart History — Every unique conversion is saved to localStorage so you can restore past sessions.
  • Dark Mode — Toggle between light and dark themes; preference is remembered across sessions.
  • Privacy First — 100% client-side. Your text never leaves your device.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a text delimiter?

A delimiter is a character or sequence of characters that separates items in a list. Common delimiters include the comma (,) used in CSV files, the pipe (|) used in TSV-style data, the semicolon (;) used in European CSV formats, and the slash (/) used in URL paths.

How do I convert comma-separated values to one item per line?

Paste your comma-separated text into the Input box, select the Comma delimiter, and click Convert. The tool automatically detects that your text contains commas and splits it into one item per line in the Output box.

How do I find duplicate values in a list?

Click the Duplicate Finder tab, paste your list (one item per line or comma-separated), then click the orange Dupes button to extract only the values that appear more than once.

How do I remove duplicates and keep unique values only?

In the Duplicate Finder tab, paste your list and click Remove Dupes. The tool deduplicates the entire list, keeping the first occurrence of each item and discarding the rest.

Is my data secure?

Yes. Pookie Tools is entirely client-side JavaScript. No text you enter is sent to any server. Your data stays in your browser and is optionally saved to your device's localStorage for history purposes.
